Concierge

I read through recent family reviews across Phuket's resorts. Here's the one I'd book, plus two alternatives worth a look — including one I'd steer you away from for now, and why.

Top Pick Marriott Bonvoy
JW Marriott Phuket Resort & Spa
·Mai Khao Beach, Phuket·Luxury
Minor refurbishment
The main lobby restaurant is being refreshed through December. Two alternative restaurants stay open throughout your dates.
Based on recent guest reviews
Why this one

For your family of six, this is the one I'd book. The two-bedroom suites take the grandparents and a toddler without anyone feeling on top of each other, the buffet runs a dedicated Indian counter your parents will return to every morning, and Mai Khao's beach is calm and shallow — exactly right for a 2-year-old. It sits a touch above your comfort baseline, but for a resort-anchored December trip, it earns the stretch.

Rooms

Two-bedroom family suites sleep six, with a separate kids' room; cots and bed rails on request.

Facilities

Three pools including a shaded kids' pool, a kids' club for ages 4+, and a quiet spa for the grandparents.

Food

A dedicated Indian and vegetarian breakfast counter, confirmed across recent reviews from Indian families.

Location

20 minutes from the airport on calm Mai Khao beachfront; night markets a short drive south.

Two more worth a look
Runner-up
Angsana Laguna Phuket
·Bang Tao Beach, Phuket·Comfort

Built around the island's biggest lagoon pool — the one kids never want to leave — with free shuttle boats around the resort. A strong comfort-tier choice; the main thing holding it second is a lighter Indian-food spread than your top pick.

Why it's second

A genuinely lovely family resort — the lagoon pools are the best on the island for young children, and the shuttle boats are a hit with 7-year-olds. I've placed it just behind because the Indian breakfast options are smaller, and the beach shelves a little more steeply than Mai Khao. Both manageable with a 2-year-old, but worth knowing before you choose.

Rooms

Interconnecting rooms and loft suites; cots provided, plenty of space for six.

Facilities

Vast lagoon pool, daily kids' club, and free shuttle boats around the grounds.

Food

Broad international buffet; vegetarian is well covered, Indian options lighter.

Location

Bang Tao Beach, a short walk to the Boat Avenue restaurants and weekend market.

IHG One Rewards
Holiday Inn Resort Karon Beach
·Karon Beach, Phuket·Comfort
Active refurbishment — avoid for now
Major construction is underway across the main pool and several room blocks through your dates. Recent guests report jackhammering from early morning and the main pool fenced off.
Based on recent guest reviews

On paper a solid, lively-beach family option with great kids' facilities — but recent stays have been disrupted by ongoing construction. I'd revisit this one once the work wraps.

Why I'd wait

I'd normally rank this higher — the location is lively and the kids' facilities are good. But I can't recommend it for your dates in good conscience: multiple recent families reported early-morning jackhammering and the main pool fenced off. That's exactly the kind of surprise that derails a trip with a toddler and grandparents. Genuinely worth another look once the refurbishment finishes.

Rooms

Family rooms sleep four comfortably; some blocks currently affected by works.

Facilities

Normally strong — kids' pool and club — but the main pool is closed during works.

Food

Buffet with some Indian dishes; vegetarian options available daily.

Location

Karon Beach, walkable to shops and dining — the busiest of the three areas.
